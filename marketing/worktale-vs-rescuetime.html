Worktale vs RescueTime

RescueTime monitors everything you do on your computer.
Worktale only tracks what you ship.

RescueTime

General productivity tracker

  • - Tracks all apps & websites
  • - Cloud-based — data stored on their servers
  • - Account required
  • - General productivity scoring
  • - Freemium — $9–15/mo for full features

Worktale

Developer work journal

  • + Tracks git commits only
  • + 100% local — nothing leaves your machine
  • + No account required
  • + Developer-specific metrics & insights
  • + Free forever — open source, MIT

When to Use Each

RescueTime is better if you…

  • • Want to track all computer usage across every app
  • • Need website-blocking or distraction alerts
  • • Want a productivity score across non-dev tools

Worktale is better if you…

  • • You're a developer who wants commit-based work logging
  • • Privacy matters — no data leaves your machine, ever
  • • You don't want surveillance software on your workstation
  • • You want something free, open source, and permanent

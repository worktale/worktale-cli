Lesson 6.1

What cloud adds.

WHAT YOU'LL LEARN

Exactly what the cloud does and doesn't do, so you can decide if it's worth opting in.

Cloud DOES

  • Host your public profile at worktale.dev/{username}
  • Store digests you explicitly publish
  • Generate AI-written weekly standups
  • Aggregate published digests across all your repos
  • Sync that public view between devices

Cloud does NOT

  • Sync your private daily log
  • Read commit diffs or source code
  • Back up your local data.db
  • Share AI session cost unless you publish it
  • Send any data before you log in

The cloud is designed as a publishing layer, not a sync layer. Your source of truth stays local. The cloud only knows what you tell it to publish.

Lesson 6.2

Device-code login.

WHAT YOU'LL LEARN

Link your CLI to a Worktale Cloud account without ever typing a password into the terminal.

Worktale Cloud uses OAuth-style device-code flow. You run one command, get a short code, paste it into a browser window that's already logged in.

$ worktale cloud login

⚡ Visit: https://worktale.dev/device

⚡ Your code: H4NK-9QXT

Polling... press Ctrl+C to cancel.

✓ Logged in as @yourusername

Where the token is stored

The bearer token lands in ~/.worktale/config.json under cloudToken. It's a long-lived token scoped to publish-and-profile actions for your account. Logging out on the CLI (worktale cloud logout) clears it locally; revoking on the web revokes it everywhere.

Desktop app login

In the desktop app, press Ctrl+6 to open Cloud view, click Login, and a browser window opens automatically with the code already passed along.

Lesson 6.3

Publishing digests.

WHAT YOU'LL LEARN

Send a saved digest to your public profile. Unpublish or edit later.

CLI

# Publish today's digest

$ worktale publish

# Publish a specific date

$ worktale publish --date 2026-04-15

# Generate and publish in one step

$ worktale digest && worktale publish

From the TUI

Open worktale, press p. Confirms and publishes.

From the desktop app

Digest view → Publish button, or Ctrl+Shift+P.

What gets sent

  • The digest body (markdown)
  • Date, commit count, lines added/removed, files touched
  • Top modules (folder names; no file names)
  • AI session aggregates for that day (session count, total cost, total tokens, top tools — no individual transcripts)
  • Repo slug (public-safe name; defaults to repo name, configurable)

What's NOT sent

  • Individual commit messages (only counts and aggregates)
  • Notes (they stay local unless pasted into the digest)
  • Absolute file paths
  • The repo's actual git history

Lesson 6.4

Your public profile.

WHAT YOU'LL LEARN

What visitors see at worktale.dev/{username} and how to customize it.

Your profile is one URL you can share with clients, hiring managers, or your own future self. It shows:

  • Your display name, bio, avatar, and optional links (GitHub / Twitter / website)
  • Streaks and all-time stats from published digests
  • AI session aggregates (last 90 days) — models used, total cost, top tools
  • A reverse-chronological timeline of your published digests

Privacy toggles per digest

Every digest has its own public/private toggle. From the desktop app or the web UI, you can:

  • Publish publicly (appears on your profile)
  • Publish privately (syncs across your devices but isn't on the public profile)
  • Unpublish entirely (removes from cloud, keeps local)

Lesson 6.5

AI-written standups.

WHAT YOU'LL LEARN

Generate weekly summary posts from your published digests, formatted for Slack, Markdown, or email.

$ worktale standup --days 7 --format slack

*Week of Apr 11 — 17*

Shipped the Stripe checkout flow (acme-portal), fixed 2 webhook race conditions, refactored payment handler. 23 commits, +2,804 / -891. AI assist: $6.12 on Claude Opus.

_Next_: rate-limit rollout + retention dashboard.

Formats: slack, markdown, email, html. The content comes from your published digests; the AI step summarizes them into a single post.

Use cases

  • Friday recap to your retainer client
  • Monday standup in Slack
  • Quarterly self-review email to your manager
  • Annual "what I shipped" summary for your own records

KEY INSIGHT

AI standup generation runs on the cloud side (not your local Ollama). Data sent to the standup endpoint = only the published digests you already made public. No local notes, no unpublished drafts.

What's free, what's paid, and what unlocks at each tier.

Pro

$9 / month · $99 / year

  • Everything in Free
  • Unlimited published digests
  • AI weekly standups (Slack/MD/email)
  • Multi-device sync of profile data
  • Private digest publishing

TIER 3

Lifetime

$249 · once

  • Everything in Pro, forever
  • Early access to new features

Remember: the CLI is free forever. The desktop app is free forever. Worktale Cloud Free is generous enough for most users. Pro exists for anyone who publishes daily and wants the AI-standup automation.
